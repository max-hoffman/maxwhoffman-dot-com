Frary

August 24, 2016

Max Hoffman

Back

Quaint on the outside.
Lush verdant, weims sniff.
Shiny pig guards a clean kitchen.
Oiled soapstone, rich Italian leather.

The basement is where they hide
our hoards, like secrets.
Piles of browned lightbulbs, antiques without legs,
buckets of rust, garbage-cans filled with sawdust.
Can’t move, hedged in crap.
Only the dust is reliably.

We hop along, jumping rug to rug.
We dodn’t fear ghosts or the dark,
just pockets of linoleum.
missteps grazing oxidized crust.
Having to feel reality.
We breathe again on the last step.

Every week we discard, we clean,
chipping away at the piles
so forcefully defended for so long.
When the basement looks like upstairs,
we will succeed.
