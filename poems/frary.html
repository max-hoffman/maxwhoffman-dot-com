Frary

August 24, 2016

Max Hoffman

Back

I've spent thousands of hours
watching a burning, agonized man,
Gifting fire to the world,
descending into hell.

Every meal, the same rubbery chicken.
Meat, that regrows in the bowels of frary,
to the likeness of prometheus on that rock.

I drown with salt to dull the char,
But the meat is yet cold.
The Frary Winds necessitate sweaters and pants,
despite my room sweating 100 degrees.

The man has been castrated
by those who found him offensive.
Because of the entire 50 foot mural
of the suffering man being condemned to hell
who I share four meals with a day,
His dick was my real concern.

Half of the world embraced fire,
the other scorned the man's sacrifice.
Those who refuse to be the crow,
inevitably become a feast to gore.

"Forethought"-
who knew the consequences of cleverness,
but still chose to sacrifice.
I still think half sets the the bar a little high.
